Pricing, Licensing & Engagement

Transparent answers about implementation costs, engagement models, Salesforce license selection, AppExchange apps, and how we measure the value we deliver.

10 questions answered

Do you work with nonprofits?

Yes. A significant portion of our portfolio consists of nonprofits. We are uniquely tailored to handle tight budgets, limited staffing, and high operational complexity.

How much will a typical Salesforce engagement cost?

Small projects typically run $8k–$16k. Medium and large builds run $21k–$63k+. Retainers scale from $2k–$4k/mo (small) to $8k–$12k/mo (enterprise).

Do you offer fixed-price projects, hourly blocks, or monthly retainers?

We offer all three: fixed-price for clearly defined, scope-frozen projects; hourly blocks for short-term burst work; and monthly retainers for proactive support.

What is included in an initial project estimate versus what becomes a change order?

Estimates cover discovery, configuration, testing, and training. Change orders typically stem from mid-project integration additions, messy data rescue, or major layout pivots.

Which Salesforce edition and add-ons do we need (Nonprofit Cloud, Experience Cloud, Marketing)?

It varies based on size. Small nonprofits often start on NPSP, while scaling entities utilize Nonprofit Cloud. Portals require Experience Cloud. We run license reviews to map this precisely to your use case.

What are typical ongoing license costs and recommended seat types?

Nonprofit Cloud user seats frequently scale around $1k–$1.5k/user/year depending on features and discounts. Marketing packages and portal logins add to this baseline.

Do we need third-party apps (AppExchange), and what do they cost?

Many orgs benefit from document generation (Conga/DocuSign) or payment processors (Chargent). Costs range from $500 to $20k+/yr depending on user counts.

How will you demonstrate tangible value or ROI early in a project?

We focus on deploying a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) within the first 4–8 weeks, prioritizing 1 or 2 high-impact automations that yield immediate time savings.

What references or case studies can you share for organizations like ours?

We maintain an extensive list of verified references covering donor database migrations, programmatic client portals, and automated commercial billing setups.

How do you measure project success after launch?

Success is tracked via quantitative metrics: system login frequency, hours saved through automation, error rate reduction, and stakeholder satisfaction scorecards.
