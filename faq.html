The Questions We Hear Most

These five questions cover the most critical decisions organizations face when evaluating a Salesforce partner. Read them in full — they're answered with complete transparency.

How much does a typical Salesforce engagement cost (implementation and ongoing support)?

Because every organization has a unique setup, costs vary based on your specific user count, required integrations, and data complexity. However, our typical engagements fall into these clear ranges:

  • Small Implementations / Projects: Typically $8,000–$16,000.
  • Medium to Large Platform Builds: $21,000–$63,000+, with very large builds frequently exceeding $60,000.
  • Ongoing Support Retainers: Monthly bands from $2,000–$4,000 (small) to $8,000–$12,000 (enterprise). We also offer fixed-fee project blocks.

Do you offer fixed-price projects, hourly blocks, or monthly retainers — and which is best for us?

We offer all three models to align with your project's predictability and goals:

  • Fixed-Price Projects: Best for narrow, well-specified builds with a clearly defined and "frozen" scope of work.
  • Hourly Blocks: Ideal for flexible, short-term "burst" work, discovery phases, or minor iterations.
  • Monthly Retainers (Ongoing Support): This is our most recommended model for long-term platform health. It ensures proactive maintenance, continuous optimizations, and predictable monthly costs.

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your scope certainty: fixed-price for defined projects, and blocks or retainers for ongoing maintenance and iterative growth.

What happens to our Salesforce platform if our internal Administrator leaves the company?

This is one of the most common catalysts for organizations transitioning to an external partner. If your internal Salesforce Admin resigns, we can step in immediately to provide fractional admin coverage, ensuring zero disruption to your daily workflows and data integrity.

Long-term, outsourcing your platform management to an ongoing support retainer gives your organization fractional access to an entire team of certified admins, custom developers, and solutions architects for significantly less than the overhead cost of recruiting and employing a single, full-time internal staff member.

How will you help our staff actually adopt Salesforce and stop relying on spreadsheets?

True software adoption succeeds only when the platform solves a real, everyday pain point for your team — such as eliminating a grueling, manual monthly report. We drive user adoption through a friction-free design and structured training process:

  • We build simplified homepages, custom task lists, and tailored user views so your staff only sees what they need.
  • We provide recorded Loom videos for routine daily tasks, build comprehensive documentation, and run interactive, role-based "train-the-trainer" workshops.
  • We implement change management strategies that give your users a voice during the design phase, and we track ongoing adoption post-launch using native Salesforce usage reports.

What is included in your initial project estimates versus what typically becomes a change order?

Our project estimates are comprehensive and cover the complete lifecycle of the agreed scope, including discovery, system configuration, thorough testing, basic user training, and a short post-launch warranty period.

To maintain total budget transparency, items that fall outside the initial minimum viable product (MVP) scope are handled via a formal change-order process. The most common drivers of change orders include unexpected data cleanup or unanticipated data quality issues, complex third-party integrations added mid-project, user interface redesigns beyond the initial scope, and additional custom report or dashboard requests. For enterprise-level clients, we routinely pad estimates to absorb standard scope drift.
